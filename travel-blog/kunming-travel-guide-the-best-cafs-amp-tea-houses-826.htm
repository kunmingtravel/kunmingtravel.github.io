Kunming Travel Guide: The Best Cafés & Tea Houses

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Kunming, the "Spring City" of China, is a destination that effortlessly blends natural beauty with urban charm. Beyond its stunning landscapes and vibrant culture, the city boasts a thriving café and tea house scene that caters to both locals and travelers. Whether you're a coffee enthusiast or a tea connoisseur, Kunming offers a diverse range of spots to unwind, socialize, and savor exceptional brews. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best cafés and tea houses in Kunming.

The Coffee Culture of Kunming

Kunming’s coffee scene has grown exponentially in recent years, with specialty cafés popping up across the city. Many of these spots source beans from Yunnan’s own coffee farms, which have gained international recognition for their high-quality Arabica.

1. Warmth Café

Located in the heart of Kunming, Warmth Café is a cozy spot known for its artisanal coffee and minimalist décor. The baristas here are passionate about their craft, offering everything from hand-drip pour-overs to creamy lattes. Don’t miss their signature Yunnan coffee, which highlights the region’s unique flavor profile—think fruity notes with a smooth finish.

2. Lost Garden

A favorite among expats and travelers, Lost Garden combines a café with a boutique guesthouse. The outdoor seating area, surrounded by lush greenery, makes it a perfect place to relax. Their espresso-based drinks are excellent, and they also serve delicious Western-style brunch options.

3. Maan Coffee

For those who prefer a more polished café experience, Maan Coffee offers a stylish setting with plush seating and a menu that includes both coffee and light meals. Their caramel macchiato is a crowd-pleaser, and the free Wi-Fi makes it a great spot for digital nomads.

Traditional Tea Houses: A Taste of Yunnan’s Heritage

While coffee culture is booming, tea remains an integral part of Kunming’s identity. Yunnan Province is famous for its Pu’er tea, and the city’s tea houses provide an authentic way to experience this centuries-old tradition.

1. Yunnan Tea Culture Museum (Tea House Section)

This isn’t just a place to drink tea—it’s an immersive experience. The museum’s tea house allows visitors to sample rare Pu’er teas while learning about their history and production. Knowledgeable staff guide you through tea ceremonies, making it a must-visit for tea lovers.

2. Jingmai Tea House

Tucked away in a quiet alley, Jingmai Tea House specializes in organic teas sourced directly from Jingmai Mountain. The serene atmosphere, complete with traditional wooden furnishings, makes it an ideal escape from the city’s hustle. Try their aged Pu’er for a deep, earthy flavor.

3. Tea Horse Road Teahouse

Named after the ancient trade route that transported tea from Yunnan to Tibet, this teahouse celebrates the region’s rich tea-trading history. The menu features a variety of Yunnan teas, including Dian Hong (Yunnan black tea) and delicate white teas. Pair your drink with some local snacks for the full experience.

Hidden Gems: Unique Cafés & Tea Houses

Beyond the well-known spots, Kunming has several hidden gems that offer something a little different.

1. Cat Book Café

A paradise for cat lovers, this café doubles as a bookstore and a feline sanctuary. Sip on a latte while surrounded by friendly resident cats—perfect for a laid-back afternoon.

2. The French Bakery & Café

Though not traditionally Chinese, this charming café serves some of the best pastries in Kunming. Their croissants are buttery and flaky, and the coffee is consistently excellent. It’s a little slice of Europe in the heart of Yunnan.

3. Bamboo Forest Tea House

For a truly tranquil experience, head to this tea house nestled in a bamboo grove on the outskirts of Kunming. The sound of rustling leaves and the gentle aroma of brewing tea create a meditative ambiance.

Tips for Enjoying Kunming’s Café & Tea Scene

  • Timing Matters: Tea houses are often busiest in the late afternoon, while cafés see a morning rush. Plan accordingly for a quieter experience.
  • Ask for Recommendations: Baristas and tea masters are usually happy to suggest their personal favorites.
  • Try Local Specialties: Don’t leave without tasting Yunnan coffee or Pu’er tea—they’re what make Kunming’s beverage scene unique.

Kunming’s cafés and tea houses are more than just places to drink—they’re gateways to the city’s culture, history, and laid-back lifestyle. Whether you’re seeking a caffeine boost or a moment of zen, you’ll find it here.
