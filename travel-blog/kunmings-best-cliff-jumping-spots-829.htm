Kunming’s Best Cliff Jumping Spots

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Kunming, the "Spring City" of China, is famous for its mild climate, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture. But beyond its bustling markets and serene lakes lies an adrenaline-pumping secret: some of the most exhilarating cliff jumping spots in Yunnan. Whether you're a seasoned thrill-seeker or a first-time jumper, Kunming’s crystal-clear waters and dramatic cliffs offer the perfect playground. Here’s your ultimate guide to the best cliff diving locations around Kunming.

The Thrill of Cliff Jumping in Kunming

Cliff jumping isn’t just about the leap—it’s about the rush of free-falling, the splash of cool water, and the unbeatable views. Kunming’s natural scenery, with its karst formations and emerald lakes, makes it an ideal destination for this extreme sport. The best part? Many of these spots remain under the radar, offering a raw, unfiltered adventure.

Safety First: Tips Before You Jump

Before we dive into the locations, let’s cover some essential safety tips:
- Check the depth: Always ensure the water is deep enough (at least 4-5 meters) to avoid injury.
- Scout the landing: Look for submerged rocks or strong currents before jumping.
- Start small: If you're new to cliff jumping, begin with lower heights and work your way up.
- Jump feet-first: This minimizes the risk of injury compared to diving headfirst.
- Bring a buddy: Never jump alone—having someone nearby can be a lifesaver in emergencies.

Now, let’s explore Kunming’s top cliff jumping spots!

1. Fuxian Lake: The Deep Blue Paradise

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Fuxian Lake, about an hour’s drive from Kunming, is one of China’s deepest freshwater lakes. Its clear, turquoise waters and towering cliffs make it a prime spot for cliff jumping.

Best Jumping Points

  • Luchong Peninsula: This area features cliffs ranging from 5 to 12 meters, perfect for both beginners and experts. The water is exceptionally clear, so you can see the depths below as you leap.
  • Gushan (Lonely Hill): A secluded spot with a 10-meter jump, offering stunning views of the lake.

Pro Tip

Visit in early summer when the water is warm but the crowds are still thin.

2. Jiuxiang Scenic Area: Cliff Jumping Meets Caves

Why It’s Unique

Jiuxiang is famous for its underground rivers and karst caves, but few know about its hidden cliff jumping spots near the waterfalls.

Best Jumping Points

  • Diehong Bridge Area: A 7-meter jump into a deep pool surrounded by lush greenery. The sound of the nearby waterfall adds to the thrill.
  • Underground River Exit: For the bold, this spot offers a 9-meter drop into a crystal-clear cave pool.

Pro Tip

Wear water shoes—the rocks can be slippery!

3. Stone Forest (Shilin): Jump Amidst Ancient Karst Towers

Why It’s Epic

The Stone Forest’s surreal limestone formations aren’t just for photos—some of its hidden pools are perfect for cliff jumping.

Best Jumping Points

  • Hidden Pool near Naigu Stone Forest: A lesser-known spot with a 6-meter jump, surrounded by towering stone pillars.
  • Long Lake (Changhu): A quieter area with cliffs up to 8 meters high.

Pro Tip

Combine your jump with a hike through the Stone Forest for a full day of adventure.

4. Dianchi Lake’s Secret Cliffs

Why It’s a Local Favorite

While Dianchi Lake is a popular tourist spot, few venture to its western shores, where rugged cliffs offer thrilling jumps.

Best Jumping Points

  • Xishan (Western Hills): Near the Dragon Gate, you’ll find cliffs ranging from 5 to 15 meters. The higher jumps are for experienced divers only.
  • Haigeng Park: A more accessible spot with lower cliffs, great for beginners.

Pro Tip

Visit at sunset for a magical leap with golden skies reflecting off the water.

5. Qiongzhu Temple Area: A Spiritual Leap

Why It’s Unforgettable

Near the ancient Qiongzhu Temple, this spot combines adrenaline with tranquility.

Best Jumping Points

  • Bamboo Forest Cliff: A 7-meter jump into a serene pool, surrounded by bamboo groves.
  • Temple Backdrop Jump: A 10-meter cliff with a view of the temple’s rooftops—perfect for an Instagram-worthy shot.

Pro Tip

Respect the temple’s peaceful atmosphere—keep noise levels down.

Cliff Jumping Events and Local Culture

Kunming’s adventure scene is growing, with local groups organizing cliff jumping meetups. Look for events on platforms like Douban or WeChat groups. Some hostels, like The Hump Kunming, also arrange group trips to these spots.

The Best Time to Go

  • May to October: Warm weather and stable water levels make this the ideal season.
  • Avoid monsoon season (July-August): Heavy rains can make currents dangerous.

Gear You’ll Need

  • Swimwear: Quick-dry materials are best.
  • Water shoes: Protect your feet from sharp rocks.
  • GoPro or waterproof phone case: Capture your jumps in high-definition.
  • Towel and dry bag: For post-jump comfort.

Final Thoughts

Kunming’s cliff jumping spots offer more than just a thrill—they’re a way to connect with nature in one of China’s most beautiful regions. From the depths of Fuxian Lake to the karst towers of Shilin, each location has its own unique charm. So pack your courage (and your swimsuit), and take the leap!
