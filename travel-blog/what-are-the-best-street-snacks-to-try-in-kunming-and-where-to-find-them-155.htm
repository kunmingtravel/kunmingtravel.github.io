What Are the Best Street Snacks to Try in Kunming and Where to Find Them?

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Kunming, the capital of Yunnan Province in southwestern China, is known not only for its mild climate and scenic landscapes but also for its vibrant food scene. The city offers a rich variety of street snacks that reflect the diverse cultures of Yunnan's many ethnic groups. Street food in Kunming is affordable, delicious, and an essential part of the city's charm. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, exploring Kunming's street food scene is a must-do activity.

From spicy skewers to savory noodles, Kunming’s street food is an adventure in itself. In this guide, we will explore some of the most popular street snacks in the city and where you can find them. Be sure to bring your appetite!

1. Guo Qiao Mi Xian (Crossing the Bridge Noodles)

One of the most famous dishes in Kunming, Guo Qiao Mi Xian, is a traditional noodle soup that has become a must-try for visitors to the city. The dish is known for its unique way of being served. The noodles are accompanied by a bowl of hot broth and a variety of fresh ingredients, including meat, vegetables, and herbs. What makes this dish special is that the broth is kept boiling, and diners add the ingredients themselves, allowing them to cook in the hot broth right before eating.

Where to Find It:

  • Jinbi Square: A popular spot for tourists, this area has several restaurants that serve Guo Qiao Mi Xian, where you can watch the noodles being prepared in the traditional style.
  • Dongfeng Square: Here, you can find local eateries serving various versions of this noodle dish. The area is known for its affordable and authentic options.

2. Chao Guo (Stir-Fried Rice Noodles)

Chao Guo, or stir-fried rice noodles, is another popular street food in Kunming. Made with rice noodles, vegetables, and a choice of meat or tofu, this dish is stir-fried in a wok with soy sauce and a special blend of spices. It’s savory, a bit spicy, and full of flavor, making it a satisfying street food choice.

Where to Find It:

  • Nanping Street: A bustling area filled with street vendors and small restaurants, Nanping Street is a great place to try stir-fried rice noodles. You’ll find vendors making the dish fresh to order, allowing you to enjoy it piping hot.
  • Wujiaba Night Market: Known for its wide range of street foods, Wujiaba is a great spot to sample Chao Guo in its many variations.

3. Kao Rou (Grilled Meat Skewers)

Grilled meat skewers, or Kao Rou, are a favorite among locals and visitors alike. These skewers feature various types of meat, such as chicken, beef, lamb, and even fish, that are marinated with spices and grilled over an open flame. The meat is tender, juicy, and full of flavor. Some vendors also offer vegetables and tofu skewers for those who prefer a lighter option.

Where to Find It:

  • Zhongshan Road: This street is lined with vendors selling Kao Rou. The aroma of grilling meat fills the air, and the prices are affordable, making it a great spot to enjoy this street snack.
  • East Railway Station Area: Near the train station, you'll find a variety of food stalls serving skewers. It’s a perfect stop for a quick snack after a long journey.

4. Yi Bao (Yunnan Rice Balls)

Yi Bao, or Yunnan rice balls, are a beloved snack made of glutinous rice stuffed with a variety of fillings such as pork, chicken, or mushrooms. The rice balls are then wrapped in a banana leaf and steamed until soft and fragrant. It’s a great snack that offers a perfect balance of flavors and textures, with the savory filling and the chewy rice creating a satisfying bite.

Where to Find It:

  • Kunming Food Street: This area is filled with vendors selling Yi Bao, and you’ll be able to sample different versions of the dish. It’s also a great place to try other Yunnan snacks.
  • Chenggong District: For a more local experience, head to Chenggong, where street vendors sell Yi Bao as part of the region's traditional food offerings.

5. Mixian (Rice Noodles in Sour Soup)

Mixian, or rice noodles in sour soup, is a spicy and tangy dish that features thin rice noodles served in a hot, sour broth made with tamarind, chili, and other ingredients. Often served with various toppings like pork, beef, or pickled vegetables, this dish is both refreshing and warming. The broth is the highlight, with its distinct sourness that will tantalize your taste buds.

Where to Find It:

  • Nanping Pedestrian Street: You’ll find street vendors selling Mixian in many spots along this bustling street. The dish is often served in a bowl with fresh herbs and a variety of meats.
  • Xiamao Night Market: This night market is famous for its variety of snacks, including Mixian, which is a great choice for a late-night snack.

6. Dian Zhi (Yunnan-Style Sausages)

Dian Zhi are Yunnan-style sausages, which are different from the sausages you might be familiar with in other parts of the world. These sausages are made with pork and seasoned with a variety of herbs and spices, giving them a unique flavor. They are usually grilled or fried and served on skewers, making them a convenient and delicious street food option.

Where to Find It:

  • Kunming Night Markets: Various night markets in Kunming offer Dian Zhi. The sausages are typically served hot and fresh, making them a perfect snack for a night out.
  • Wangfujing Street: Known for its many street food vendors, Wangfujing offers a range of grilled meats, including Dian Zhi.

7. Pipa Tofu (Grilled Tofu)

Pipa Tofu is a popular street snack in Kunming, made by grilling tofu that has been marinated with a blend of spices. The tofu is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, with a rich, smoky flavor. It’s often served with a sprinkling of chili flakes, garlic, and coriander, making it a flavorful and satisfying snack for vegetarians and meat-eaters alike.

Where to Find It:

  • Kunming's Central Markets: Many central markets, such as the Yunnan Folk Culture Market, offer Pipa Tofu as one of their street food options.
  • Renmin Road: Known for its street food stalls, Renmin Road is another great place to find this popular grilled tofu dish.

8. Xianyu (Salty Fish)

Xianyu, or salty fish, is another traditional snack found in Kunming. The fish is preserved through salting and drying, giving it a strong, salty flavor. It is often served with a variety of dips, pickled vegetables, or eaten on its own. The strong flavor of the fish is balanced with refreshing side dishes, making it a great snack to accompany a cold drink.

Where to Find It:

  • Kunming Food Streets: Many food streets offer salty fish as a local delicacy. The best places to find it are near food stalls or small family-run eateries.
  • Jiuxiang Caves Area: In the Jiuxiang area, you can find street vendors selling salty fish, often served with pickled vegetables and rice.

9. Douhua (Tofu Pudding)

Douhua, or tofu pudding, is a sweet and silky dessert made from soft tofu and served with a syrupy sweet sauce. Sometimes, you can find variations that include toppings like red beans, peanuts, or sesame seeds. This dessert is light and refreshing, making it a perfect way to end a street food tour in Kunming.

Where to Find It:

  • Kunming Street Food Markets: Douhua is often served at street food markets and can be found alongside other traditional snacks.
  • Donghua Jie: A lively street known for its mix of street food offerings, Donghua Jie offers a great place to try Douhua.

Kunming’s street food scene is as diverse as its culture, offering a blend of flavors, textures, and spices that will leave you craving more. Whether you're enjoying a bowl of hot noodles or savoring grilled skewers, each dish tells a story about the city’s rich culinary heritage. With this guide, you’ll be ready to embark on a street food adventure in Kunming, experiencing some of the best snacks the city has to offer. Make sure to explore local food streets, night markets, and popular spots where locals gather, as these are the places where the most authentic and delicious snacks can be found.
