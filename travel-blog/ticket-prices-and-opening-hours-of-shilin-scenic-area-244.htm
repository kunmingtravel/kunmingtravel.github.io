Ticket Prices and Opening Hours of Shilin Scenic Area

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Shilin Scenic Area, also known as the Stone Forest, is one of China’s most famous natural wonders, located in Yunnan Province, about 90 kilometers from Kunming. This breathtaking karst landscape features towering limestone formations, caves, waterfalls, and lakes, making it a popular destination for both domestic and international travelers. If you’re planning a visit, it’s essential to understand the ticket pricing structure, opening hours, and additional costs that might come with the trip.

Ticket Prices for Shilin Scenic Area

The entrance fee to Shilin Scenic Area varies depending on the season, attractions included, and possible discounts for certain groups of visitors. Below is a breakdown of the ticket prices:

Standard Ticket Prices

  • General Admission: ¥130 per person
  • Discounted Ticket (Students, seniors, children under 1.2 meters): ¥65 per person
  • Children under 1.2 meters: Free

This standard ticket includes entry to the main scenic area, where you can explore famous spots like Greater Stone Forest (Da Shilin), Lesser Stone Forest (Xiao Shilin), Lizi Yuanjing, and Shilin Lake.

Additional Attraction Fees

Apart from the main park, there are several other attractions within the larger Stone Forest area that may require separate tickets:

  • Naigu Stone Forest (Naigu Shilin): ¥25 per person
  • Changhu Lake (Long Lake): ¥10 per person
  • Dadu Lake (Moon Lake): ¥15 per person
  • Shilin Karst Geological Museum: Free

Tourist Shuttle Fees

For visitors who prefer not to walk long distances, there is an electric shuttle bus service within the park:

  • One-way shuttle ride: ¥25 per person
  • Round-trip shuttle ride: ¥40 per person

This shuttle service is useful for reaching different sections of the park, especially if you want to explore multiple areas without spending too much time walking.

Guided Tours and Other Costs

  • English-speaking tour guide: ¥200 – ¥500 (depending on the tour duration)
  • Audio guide rental: ¥20 – ¥50 per device
  • Photography service: ¥50 – ¥200 (professional photos in ethnic clothing)

For an immersive experience, hiring a local tour guide can be beneficial, especially to learn about the myths and legends behind the unique rock formations.

Opening Hours of Shilin Scenic Area

The Shilin Scenic Area is open year-round, but its daily operating hours vary depending on the season.

General Opening Hours

  • Peak season (April – October): 7:30 AM – 6:30 PM
  • Low season (November – March): 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM

During national holidays such as Chinese New Year, Golden Week (October), and May Day holidays, the park can be extremely crowded, so it’s advisable to visit early in the morning to avoid long queues.

Best Time to Visit for a Comfortable Experience

  • Morning visits (7:30 AM – 10:30 AM): Best for photography and avoiding crowds.
  • Late afternoon (4:00 PM – 6:00 PM): Ideal for catching the golden sunset over the stone formations.
  • Avoid midday (11:00 AM – 3:00 PM): This is the busiest time, and the sun can be quite intense.

How to Buy Tickets for Shilin Scenic Area

There are several ways to purchase tickets for the Stone Forest:

1. Online Booking

  • Official websites, such as Shilin Scenic Area’s official site or China’s major travel platforms (Ctrip, Meituan, Qunar, etc.), allow advance booking.
  • Online prices may sometimes include discounts or combo deals for additional attractions.
  • Most platforms accept mobile payment methods such as Alipay and WeChat Pay.

2. On-Site Purchase

  • Tickets can be bought at the entrance of the scenic area.
  • Payment methods include cash, credit cards, and mobile payments.
  • During peak seasons, the lines for ticket purchases can be long, so early arrival is recommended.

3. Through Tour Agencies

  • Many tour companies in Kunming offer day trips to Shilin, which often include transportation, guided tours, and entrance tickets.
  • Prices vary depending on the package and group size, typically ranging from ¥250 – ¥500 per person.

Other Important Travel Tips

1. Best Seasons to Visit

  • Spring (March – May): Comfortable temperatures, lush greenery, and blooming flowers.
  • Autumn (September – November): Cool and dry, perfect for hiking and sightseeing.
  • Winter (December – February): Less crowded but can be chilly, with occasional mist adding a mystical feel.

Avoid visiting during the summer (June – August) because of high humidity and frequent rain showers.

2. How to Get to Shilin Scenic Area

  • From Kunming by High-Speed Train:

    • Take the Kunming – Shilin West high-speed train (about 30 minutes).
    • From Shilin West Station, take a local bus or taxi to the scenic area (about 15 minutes).

  • From Kunming by Bus:

    • Kunming East Bus Station offers direct buses to Shilin Scenic Area.
    • The journey takes 1.5 – 2 hours and costs around ¥30 – ¥50 per person.

  • By Private Car or Taxi:

    • Hiring a private driver from Kunming costs around ¥300 – ¥500 for a round trip.

3. What to Bring for the Trip

  • Comfortable shoes (lots of walking required)
  • Sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses (for sunny days)
  • Raincoat or umbrella (weather can change quickly)
  • Snacks and water (limited food options inside the park)
  • Camera or smartphone (to capture the stunning rock formations)

4. Local Culture and Traditions

Shilin is home to the Yi ethnic minority, and visitors may see locals dressed in traditional Yi costumes. The Torch Festival, held in July or August, is a spectacular cultural event featuring bonfires, dances, and traditional performances.

Shilin Scenic Area is undoubtedly one of China’s most unique natural landscapes, and knowing the ticket prices, opening hours, and travel details will help you plan a smooth and memorable visit. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast, a photographer, or simply someone looking to explore one of Yunnan’s most famous attractions, Shilin promises an unforgettable experience.
